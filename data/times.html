How India can use its unique position at Hiroshima G7

  • Manjeev Singh Puri
  • May 18, 2023, 21:03 IST

India is participating in the G7 summit in Japan and it holds the G20 presidency. This underlines how strongly it is placed to promote global collaboration in a rules-based order

Prime Minister Narendra Modi is travelling to Hiroshima , Japan , to participate in the 49th G7 summit being held on May 19-21. This would be the fourth time that he would be attending a G7 summit. For India, this will be the 10th participation.
G7 has a practice of outreach by interacting with a select number of invited countries decided by the host. This began in 2003 when PM Atal Bihari Vajpayee participated in the G7 summit in France.
